### Implementation Details

1. **Dashboard Layout**:
   - Organized as a top-level vertical concatenation (`vconcat`) of 2 items:
     - First item: View A (the 2D brush host scatter plot).
     - Second item: A horizontal concatenation (`hconcat`) of View B (genre bar chart) and View C (IMDb rating histogram).
   - This matches the layout: `scatter & (bar | hist)`.

2. **Crossfilter Brush Selection**:
   - Created an interval parameter named `"brush"` on the scatter plot.
   - Tied scatter plot point colors to the brush state: points inside the brush are colored by `Major_Genre:N`, and points outside are colored a flat `'lightgray'`.
   - Applied the brush selection as a filter (`transform_filter(brush)`) on both the bar chart and the histogram views, enabling dynamic updates as the brush is dragged.

3. **Data Pre-filtering**:
   - Added `transform_filter` transforms to every view to exclude null `IMDB_Rating` values.
   - Added an additional `transform_filter` to the scatter plot to exclude null `Rotten_Tomatoes_Rating` values, ensuring only complete records reach each view.
   - Filtered out null `Major_Genre` values in the bar chart to keep the visualization clean.

4. **Top 8 Genres Filtering**:
   - Used a `transform_joinaggregate` to compute the total counts per genre, followed by a `transform_window` with `dense_rank` to dynamically identify and restrict the bar chart to the top 8 most frequent movie genres globally. This keeps the layout compact and prevents the y-axis from jumping around during brushing.
