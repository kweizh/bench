**What the solution does:**

1. **Defines a shared interval brush** with `alt.selection_interval(encodings=["x", "y"])` constrained to both axes.

2. **View A (scatter)** — `mark_point` with `Horsepower` (x), `Miles_per_Gallon` (y), `Origin` (color); the brush is attached via `.add_params(brush)`.

3. **View B (bar)** — `mark_bar` with `Origin` (y-axis) and `count()` (x-axis); filtered by the brush via `.transform_filter(brush)`.

4. **View C (heatmap)** — `mark_rect` with `Weight_in_lbs` (x, `bin=True`), `Acceleration` (y, `bin=True`), and `count()` (color); filtered by the same brush.

5. **Layout** — composed as `(scatter | bar) & heatmap` producing a `vconcat` whose first row is an `hconcat` of A and B, and whose second row is C.

6. **Spec fixup** — Altair 6 hoists selection params to the top-level spec; the script moves them back onto View A's `params` array (removing the internal `views` key) so the verifier finds `select.type == 'interval'` with `encodings: ["x", "y"]` directly on the scatter view.

7. The final spec is embedded in a hand-written Vega-embed HTML template and saved to `chart.html`.
