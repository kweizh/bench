### What `build_chart.py` does

1. **Reads** `ohlcv.csv` with pandas.
2. **Defines** an `alt.selection_interval(encodings=["x"], name="brush")` parameter.
3. **Upper candlestick view** — a layered chart:
   - `mark_rule` with `y=low` / `y2=high` (wick)
   - `mark_bar` with `y=open` / `y2=close` (body)
   - Both layers use `alt.condition("datum.open <= datum.close", alt.value("#26a69a"), alt.value("#ef5350"))` for bullish (teal) / bearish (red) colouring
   - The `x` channel carries `scale=alt.Scale(domain=brush)` so brushing the lower panel zooms/pans this view
4. **Lower volume view** — `mark_bar` of `volume` over `date:T`, with `add_params(brush)` to host the interval selection.
5. **Vertical composition** via `alt.vconcat`.
6. **Post-processes the spec dict** to move the brush param from the Altair-6-hoisted top-level back into `vconcat[1].params`, so the acceptance criterion ("selection appears in the lower view's params") is satisfied.
7. Writes `chart.json` (the corrected spec) and `chart.html` (a hand-built standalone page with `vegaEmbed`).
