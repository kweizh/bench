## Quarterly Operations Summary

ACME Robotics delivered 1,284 industrial units in Q3 2026, an increase of 18.6% year-over-year. Manufacturing throughput rose to 94.2% utilization, exceeding the 88% target set in March. Net revenue from the robotics segment reached $42.7M, while operating costs held steady at $28.1M, yielding a 34.2% margin. Customer satisfaction surveys returned an average score of 4.7/5 across 312 enterprise accounts in North America and Europe.

# Research and Development Highlights

The vision-systems team open-sourced its calibration toolkit and published two peer-reviewed papers on neural depth estimation. Prototype build P-44 achieved 0.18 mm positioning accuracy under stress tests, surpassing the design specification of 0.25 mm. The autonomous-navigation working group migrated to ROS 2 Iron and reduced average planning latency from 142 ms to 87 ms. Twelve provisional patents were filed during the quarter.

# Outlook and Risks

We expect Q4 deliveries to grow 12-15% sequentially as the new Tucson assembly line ramps to full capacity. Supply-chain risk remains elevated for high-precision LiDAR modules; alternate sourcing from two new vendors is being qualified this quarter. Currency headwinds from the EUR/USD pair could reduce reported revenue by approximately 1.4% if current rates persist. We reiterate full-year guidance of $165M-$172M in segment revenue.
